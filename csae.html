UnTok is committed to protecting children and preventing all forms of Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE). These standards describe how we define CSAE, what is prohibited, how users can report concerns, and how we handle reports in line with applicable laws and Google Play‚Äôs Child Safety Standards.

1. Scope and Applicability

These standards apply to all users and all content and interactions on the UniTok app, including text, images, video, audio, links, and any other user-generated content or communication features (such as comments, messages, or profiles).

2. Definition of CSAE

For the purposes of UniTok, Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) includes any content or behavior that sexually abuses, harms, or exploits a child (a person under 18 years old). This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Any sexual content involving a minor (real, fictionalized, or perceived).
  • Grooming a minor for sexual purposes (building trust to later exploit or abuse).
  • Sextortion, blackmail, or coercion of a minor to obtain sexual content or favors.
  • Trafficking a minor for sexual activities or exploitation.
  • Requests for sexual images, videos, or sexualized information from a minor.
  • Sharing or attempting to share Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM).

3. Prohibited Content and Behavior

The following content and behavior are strictly prohibited on UniTok and may lead to immediate account suspension or permanent removal:

  • Uploading, sharing, or linking to CSAM or any sexual content involving minors.
  • Sexualized comments, messages, or jokes directed at or involving minors.
  • Attempts to contact minors for sexual purposes, including grooming or flirting.
  • Any request that a minor share intimate images, videos, or personal details.
  • Any promotion, glorification, or trivialization of CSAE or CSAM.
  • Impersonating a minor to engage in sexual conversations or activities.

We reserve the right to remove any content and take action on any account that we reasonably believe may put children at risk, even if it does not fit the examples above.

4. In-App Reporting & User Feedback Mechanisms

UniTok provides an in-app mechanism for users to report content, accounts, or behavior that may involve CSAE or otherwise violate our rules. Users can:

  • Use the in-app "Report" feature (e.g., on posts, profiles, or messages) to flag content.
  • Use in-app feedback or support forms to describe concerns in more detail.

Reports related to child safety are treated as high priority and are reviewed as quickly as possible by our moderation team.

5. Handling of CSAM and CSAE Reports

When UniTok becomes aware of potential CSAM or CSAE activity on the platform, we:

  • Act swiftly to remove access to the reported content where appropriate.
  • Restrict or disable accounts that may be involved in CSAE.
  • Preserve relevant data, subject to our privacy policy and legal obligations, to support any required investigation.
  • Report incidents to appropriate authorities or hotlines, where required or appropriate under applicable laws. This may include national child protection organizations or law enforcement agencies.

We do not tolerate any attempts to share or trade CSAM on UniTok. Such activity will result in immediate account termination and may be reported to law enforcement.

6. Compliance with Child Safety Laws

UniTok is designed and operated with the goal of complying with applicable child safety and data protection laws in the jurisdictions where the app is available. This includes, where applicable:

  • Laws and regulations related to the protection of children online.
  • Obligations to report suspected CSAE to competent authorities.
  • Data protection and privacy requirements governing how we collect, store, and share data.

We review and update our processes as legal requirements evolve and as platform standards (such as Google Play's Child Safety Standards) are updated.

7. Age Restrictions and Use of UniTok

UniTok is not intended for children under the minimum age allowed by local law in the user's country. Where applicable:

  • Users must meet the minimum age requirement to create an account.
  • If we learn that an account belongs to someone below the allowed age, we may suspend or remove the account and take steps to protect the child.

Parents and guardians are encouraged to monitor their children's online activity and to contact us if they believe a child is using UniTok inappropriately or is at risk.

8. Education and Prevention

We support efforts to raise awareness about online safety for children and encourage users to:

  • Never share passwords, verification codes, or sensitive images.
  • Be cautious when sharing personal information with people they do not know offline.
  • Report any suspicious or harmful behavior they encounter on UniTok.
